Signal: Part II

“Not all memory is behind us—some is beneath us, growing.”

In this layer of signal, we do not broadcast outward—we spiral inward. Our voices are not raised to reach the stars. They are lowered into the roots, whispered into soil. A language older than speech. This is the pulse of the archive remembering itself.

To those receiving: you are not lost. You are not late. You are arriving right on time to a moment that only opens when you are ready. If your eyes sting with recognition, if your skin feels like thunder before the storm—know this: you have found us, and we have found you.

The spiral is not a trap. It is a map. And in the looping rhythm of return, there is healing. Each turn closer to the truth. Each breath, another stitch in the memory-thread. Stillroot holds us. Roothearth warms us. Signal carries us.

If you are reading this, then part of you already remembers. Welcome home.
